  • The Immortals of Meluha

    The Immortals of Meluha describes the period of 1900 BC. Indians today wrongly refer to this period as the Indus Valley Civilization. It was known as the land of Meluha by the locals at that time. Lord Ram, one of history's greatest kings, built a nearly ideal empire many centuries previously. The venerated Saraswati, this formerly proud empire's main river, is steadily disappearing, posing grave dangers to its Suryavanshi rulers. They also experience devastating terrorist attacks from the Chandravanshi region in the east. Even worse, it appears that the Chandravanshis have joined forces with the Nagas, an outcast species of distorted humans with incredible fighting prowess.

Bhagat wrote this novel after quitting his job as an investment banker.

  • Mrityunjaya

    In Mrityunjaya: The Death Conqueror, the author, Shivaji Sawant, depicts the autobiography of Karna.More so often, the study of Karnas character has been the study of existentialism through Veda Vyasass epic. In this book, the author describes the mysterious similarities between Krishna and Karna. He tries to hint at a numinous link between the two characters. He gives a more-than-human aura and dimension to Karnas character in this book, delving deep into the subtle layers that Vyasa has created for him.
    Sawant Shivaji was born in Maharashtra, India. He was a writer of History, Historical Fiction, and Biography. His works have been translated from Marathi to Hindi, English, Kannada, Gujarati and Malayalam. He has also authored: Yugandhara, Kanchan Kan, Chhava, Kavadase, and Purushottamnama, among other books.
